The future of pediatric development and disability

An authority on childhood behavioral development explores our evolving understanding of related disabilities and the latest approaches to care.
April 18, 2025
|
By Stanford Engineering Staff
This article is part of the series: 
The Future of Everything Podcast
One of the keys to healthy brain development may be skin-to-skin contact between infants and their caregivers. | Shutterstock/Bricolage

Physician and psychologist Heidi Feldman is a pioneer in the field of developmental behavioral pediatrics who says that the world’s understanding of childhood disability is changing and so too are the ways we approach it. 

Where once institutionalization was common, today we find integrative, family-centered approaches, charting a more humane, hopeful path forward. For example, for children born prematurely with increased likelihood of disability, increasing skin-to-skin contact – what is called  “kangaroo care” – can literally reshape that child’s brain development, she tells host Russ Altman on this episode of Stanford Engineering’s The Future of Everything podcast.
