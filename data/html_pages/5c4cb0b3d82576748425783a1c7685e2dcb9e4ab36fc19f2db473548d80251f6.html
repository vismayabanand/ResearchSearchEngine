Stanford Law School Professor Anne Joseph O'Connell authored an article, "Can the President Appoint Principal Officers Without the Senate?," published by Lawfare. She addresses President Trump's assertions when it comes to selecting principal officers, and removing inferior ...officers not picked by the president. In her piece, O'Connell specifically focuses on these hiring and firing claims.

"The White House claims that the president’s authority to fire these Senate-confirmed presidential picks—even in the face of statutory removal or notice protections—rests on Article II of the Constitution," O'Connell said.

Lightbox link for post with description Stanford Law Professor Julian Nyarko calls...

Stanford Law Professor Julian Nyarko calls contracts “the invisible infrastructure of the economy.” But for all their importance, they’ve remained remarkably difficult to access and study in the aggregate. A new Stanford Law project is changing that. The new Material Contracts Corpus is a ...first-of-its-kind, publicly accessible dataset containing more than a million contracts filed by public companies with the SEC between 2000 and 2023. The MCC transforms decades of company filings into a richly annotated, machine-readable dataset, making systematic empirical analysis of contract language both possible and easily accessible for the first time.

“By opening access to over one million high-stakes agreements, we hope the MCC gives scholars, practitioners, and policymakers a way to study how the legal foundations of commerce evolve in real time,” said Nyarko, who is also an associate director and senior fellow at the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI).
