Affordable Pathways at UC Riverside

Most Affordable in Southern California

Every out-of-state student, regardless of income, who dreams big and works hard should be able to graduate from the greatest public university system in the world! Let us show you how to pay for school as a Highlander at the University of California, Riverside (UCR).

How to Make UC Riverside Affordable

Non-Resident Achievement Scholarship

Out-of-state students are automatically considered for a scholarship, as long as you maintain a 3.0 while attending UCR!

Learn More >>

Veterans

Student veterans, dependents, or other military-affiliated students may have access to other financial benefits to help pay for their UC Riverside education!

Learn More >>

Deferred Payment Plan

Students don't necessarily need to pay all their tuition and fees at one time. They can request a special payment plan that spreads out their payments across the quarter.

Learn More >>

Career Center

Get career-ready with drop-In advising, resume workshops, graduate school preparation, career fairs, jobs, and internships.

Learn More >>

University of California Tuition Stability Plan

As an incoming UCR Highlander, your tuition will remain flat until you graduate, for up to six years! This makes it easier for you to plan for the cost of achieving a UC degree.

Ready to apply for financial aid?

The very first thing you need to is complete your FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) by the March 2 priority deadline. Even if you don’t think you may receive much aid, complete this anyways to be eligible!

Complete Your FAFSA >>

For an out-of-state student, here’s how Summer Sessions can help you save:

  • You pay in-state tuition during summer!
  • All UCR undergraduates only pay for the first eight units, but you can take more for nearly free!
  • If you’re staying in campus apartments, your summer rent could be free!
 

Consider Summer Sessions

Scholarships

There are scholarships available that out-of-state students can apply for, including from UCR and from all sorts of outside agencies, businesses, or non-profits. You can apply for as many scholarships as you want and many of them, you might automatically be considered for once you’re admitted!

 

See what you might be eligible for!

Lower Cost of Living

The cost of living in Riverside is much lower than metropolitan areas like Los Angeles, Orange County, and San Diego. This makes renting or sharing an off-campus apartment or house a lot more affordable.
