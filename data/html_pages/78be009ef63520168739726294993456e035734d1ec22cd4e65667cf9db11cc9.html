Congratulations, Class of 2025!

We are thrilled to welcome everyone to our in-person CS Commencement Ceremony!

Event Details

  • Date: Sunday, June 15, 2025
  • Estimated Duration: 1pm-3pm (subject to change; see schedule below)
    • 11:00 AM – Doors open to the public
    • 11:30 AM – Lunch available in The Grove
    • 12:45 PM – Graduates should be fully checked in and seated
    • 1:00 PM – Ceremony begins
  • Location: Frost Amphitheater (351 Lasuen St., Stanford, CA 94305)
    • Parking: Spots have been reserved and are available on the 3rd and 4th lower levels of the Knight Management Garage for attendees of the CS Commencement Ceremony (655 Knight Way, Stanford, CA 94305).
      • Posted signs on the 3rd and 4th levels will read "Reserved for 2025 Computer Science Commencement Ceremony ONLY".
      • Please note that these spots are on a first-come first-served basis, as spots are limited.
  • Important Note: Tickets are not required for entry. However, graduates and their guests must present a lunch ticket in order to receive lunch. To receive lunch tickets, graduates must register their guest information on the Tassel website. Tickets will be available for pick-up at the Gates Computer Science Building lobby.
    • Lunch Ticket Pick-Up Dates:
      • Tuesday, May 27th, 2025 from 8am-10am
      • Wednesday, May 28th, 2025 from 2pm-4pm
      • Thursday, May 29th, 2025 from 10am-12pm
      • Friday, May 30th, 2025 from 11am-1pm
      • Monday, June 2nd, 2025 from 10am-12pm
      • Tuesday, June 3rd, 2025 from 8am-10am
      • Wednesday, June 4th, 2025 from 2pm-4pm
      • Thursday, June 5th, 2025 from 10am-12pm
      • Friday, June 6th, 2025 from 11am-1pm
      • Monday, June 9th, 2025 from 10am-12pm
    • Thursday, May 29, 2025 (12:00 AM PST) - Tassel Registration Form closes
       

General Terms & Conditions for ALL Attendees

Entering the venue shall be deemed consenting to ALL the General Terms & Conditions (below) and any other conditions set by Stanford University at its events.   

  • For students: Students should arrive with their gowns in hand (not worn) until they pass security checkpoints. Once inside, students may put on their gowns. A current Stanford photo ID is required for all participating graduates for check-in.
  • For all guests: Please read the list below in detail for ALL approved/unapproved items.
    • Permitted Bags
      • Limited to one bag per person
      • Clear plastic, vinyl, or PVC bags, backpacks, or fanny packs that do not exceed 12" x 12" x 6".
      • One-gallon clear plastic bags (Ziploc bag or similar).
      • Small clutch bags, purses, or fanny packs no larger than 6" x 9" (approximately the size of a hand), with or without a handle or strap.
      • Dedicated hydration packs no larger than 12"x12"x6" with no more than 2 pockets (one for the reservoir and one additional pocket). Hydration bladder is limited to 2L and must be empty prior to entry. Hydration pack does not need to be clear.
    • The following is NOT permitted INSIDE the venue:
      • Amplification equipment, airhorns, or artificial noisemakers of any kind of any kind
      • Signs, flyer distribution, banners, stickers, or posters of any kind
      • Flags, poles, or totems, of any kind
      • Any bags larger than 12”x12”x6, non-clear bags larger than 6”x9” (approximately the size of a hand) or any other bag not approved in the “clear bag” policy
      • Reusable water bottles larger than 1L or 32oz (must be empty prior to entry).
      • Outside alcoholic beverages
      • Any outside beverages other than factory-sealed water in plastic bottles up to 32oz or 1L.
      • Hydration packs which have more than 2 pockets (one for the reservoir and one additional pocket). Hydration bladder is limited to 2L and must be empty prior to entry.
      • Liquid of any kind which is not in a factory-sealed plastic bottle.
      • Blankets, towels or tapestries larger than 4.5'x6'
      • Tarps regardless of size
      • Chairs of any kind
      • Cameras with detachable lenses and/or any lenses that extend to more than 6"
      • Audio or video recording devices
      • GoPros, selfie-sticks, monopods or tripods
      • Aerosol spray cans (including sunscreen or bug spray)
      • Balloons, streamers or confetti cannons/poppers
      • Balls, frisbees, whole fruits/vegetables (unless cut into small pieces) and other potential projectiles
      • Bicycles, scooters, skateboards, hoverboards, roller skates, roller blades, and roller shoes
      • Remote controlled devices of any kind (drones or other aircraft, cars or other toys)
      • Coolers or picnic baskets of any kind
      • Cutlery or metal silverware of any kind
      • Glass or ceramic of any kind, including bottles, jars, containers, etc.
      • Costumes that resemble public safety uniforms (police, fire, security or medical, including reflective or hi-viz vests or straps) in any way
      • Full face masks or other full face coverings, including costumes that include them
      • Fireworks or any incendiary devices
      • Glow sticks, light sticks, flow toys and Poi Balls, Poi Sticks, or any other battery-operated light larger than 2" long
      • LED apparel, toys, gloves or other wearables or accessories of any kind
      • Inflatables of any kind (including wind chairs, air couches, pool floats, pool tubes and inflatable costumes)
      • Hula hoops
      • Laptops or tablets
      • Laser pointers
      • Markers or spray paint
      • Pepper spray
      • Strollers, baby carriers or wagons
      • Tents and tables of any kind
      • Umbrellas, parasols, sun shelters or shade structures
      • Weapons or sharp objects of any kind
      • All tobacco products. Smoking or vaping is not allowed anywhere in the venue.

Any attendee causing disruptions or found with prohibited (or otherwise restricted) items may be subject to removal from the venue.   

  • Livestream (via YouTube) and filming will occur at this event: This event will be photographed, and webcast live. Official photographs may be taken by staff for posting on Stanford websites and may be used in other university publications. By entering this event venue, you consent to Stanford University using your image and likeness.

Important Updates

CS Student Services Reminder

  • Interested in participating in the CS Commencement Ceremony? The CS Commencement Ceremony Interest form accepted its last submissions on the May 2nd (5 pm PST) deadline. If you are interested in participating in the CS Department Commencement Ceremony, you will need to complete this Late Request Interest Form (via SmartSheet) by the May 14th 2025, 5pm (PST) deadline to be considered. Since we have a high influx of requests, it may take an extended amount of time to inform you of your attendance status. Your patience is appreciated.
    • Important Note: Once the CS Commencement Ceremony Interest form is closed, it will no longer take any submissions.
      • This form only serves to let the ceremony organizers know about your interest in participating in the CS Commencement Ceremony and does NOT guarantee participation. The CS Student Services team will update you regarding your attendance after reviewing your submission. Students who are confirmed to participate in the CS Commencement Ceremony (before/by the deadline) will be notified.
      • Please look out for any communications from SmartSheet, CS ceremony team, or the CS Student Services team.
  • You may attend the CS Commencement Ceremony, if you are:
    • Applying to graduate in Axess (and have received approval on your program sheet) or;
    • Participating as a walk-through student (i.e. students who are not applying to graduate and have met the eligibility requirements). To participate as a walk-through student, at least one of these should apply to you:
      • BS students must have completed 160 undergraduate units
      • MS students must have completed 36 graduate units
      • PhD students must have completed 135 units and all the milestones. For walk-through students, oral exams must be completed by May 23, 2025. Helen Buendicho Madrigal has emailed a survey containing all the necessary details—please ensure you submit it by the same (May 23rd) deadline.
  • Thursday, May 29, 2025 (12:00 AM PST) - Tassel Registration Form closes

Health & Safety Update

  • Public Safety Clear Bag Policy
  • We will have a limited bag check station and cannot guarantee that there will be space available for your bag. Be aware that any checked bag may be subject to inspection when checking it in.
  • All attendees are subject to security screening.

  • We follow all federal, state, and local guidelines regarding public health and safety.

Preparation

  • Graduate Check-in: Doors open at 11:00 AM. The graduate entrance will be at the Galvez Gate.
    • Check-in tables are ordered alphabetically by last name. Please bring your Stanford Student ID in order to check-in. The digital copy will suffice.
    • Graduates are required to check in and must do so by 12:30 PM. All graduates must be fully checked in and seated by 12:45 PM.
    • All other attendees must enter through the Lasuen Gate.
    • Tickets are not required for entry. However, tickets for the luncheon are required.
  • Allocate time in your schedule to account for the security screening process.
  • A simple bagged lunch will be ready for pick-up in The Grove starting at 11:30 AM. A lunch ticket must be presented for luncheon redemption.
  • To receive luncheon tickets, graduates must register both themselves and their guests using the Tassel registration form. Tickets will be available for pick-up at the Gates Computer Science Building lobby.
    • Lunch Ticket Pick-Up Dates:
      • Tuesday, May 27th, 2025 from 8am-10am
      • Wednesday, May 28th, 2025 from 2pm-4pm
      • Thursday, May 29th, 2025 from 10am-12pm
      • Friday, May 30th, 2025 from 11am-1pm
      • Monday, June 2nd, 2025 from 10am-12pm
      • Tuesday, June 3rd, 2025 from 8am-10am
      • Wednesday, June 4th, 2025 from 2pm-4pm
      • Thursday, June 5th, 2025 from 10am-12pm
