How to get involved with IDeaS

CMU Faculty

Take an active role in the annual IDeaS conference by doing any of the following

  • Organizing or attending sessions
  • Reviewing papers
  • Suggesting speakers
  • Submitting papers and/or posters
  • Running software demos
  • Recruiting attendees and paper submissions

Take an active role in the annual IDeaS summer institute by doing any of the following

  • Suggesting topics and/or speakers
  • Running a 1.5 hour tutorial
  • Having your students attend the institute

CMU Students

  • Reviewing papers
  • Suggesting speakers
  • Attending sessions
  • Submitting papers and/or posters
  • Running software demos
  • Take classes with IDeaS topics

Industry Partners

  • Attend our annual conference 
  • Attend the IDeaS Summer Institute
  • See our institutional membership options

Center for IDeaS Events

ANNUAL CONFERENCE
Held at CMU each year, this conference will attract attendees from the business and policy communities, government personnel and academics.

WORKSHOP
These one- or two-day outreach events present IDeaS research related to media, information diffusion, community resiliency and disinformation. Workshops are intended as an open forum for dialogue among scientists, practitioners and policymakers.

IDeaS SUMMER INSTITUTE
This intense, hands-on, five-day training camp teaches participants about the theories, methods and tools to identify and combat disinformation.

RESEARCH PARTNERSHIPS REVIEW
Research partners at the Gold and Platinum levels are invited to the annual IDeaS research review meeting, which will feature presentations on key research. These members will also have the opportunity to set research direction with IDeaS leadership by reviewing proposals that have been submitted and voting for those they would like to see funded. Platinum members will have a seat on the advisory board, which will meet during the review.
