The Hammer Museum at UCLA believes in the promise of art and ideas to illuminate our lives and build a more just world.

On View

Performance on Paper

  • May 3 – Aug 10, 2025
Featuring prints and drawings created at the intersection of music and dance by some twenty artists active from the 1960s to the present, this exhibition explores how works of art on paper can store sound and movement, becoming lasting visual records of ephemeral sonic and dance experiences.

HEAD FOR THE HILLS! Selections from the Grunwald Center and Hammer Contemporary Collection

  • Feb 9 – Aug 24, 2025
Including many recent acquisitions and demonstrating the Hammer’s commitment to collecting diverse work by local and international artists, this exhibition highlights artworks that use the local, personal, and banal to tap into the universal.

Hammer Projects: Lap-See Lam

  • Feb 1 – Sep 7, 2025
Lap-See Lam’s immersive video installation Tales of the Altersea is inspired by the ruins of a dragon-shaped ship that Lam could see from her studio window in art school. The ruins of this historic vessel serve as a jumping-off point for Lam’s dreamlike story of loss, memory, and resilience.

Hammer Projects: Colectivo Cherani

  • Feb 9 – Aug 31, 2025
As part of a 2011 political uprising by the Purépecha community of Cherán, in Michoacán, Mexico, Colectivo Cherani has pioneered a cultural movement that seeks to recover and invigorate the plurality of cultural expressions of the Purépecha people.

Armand Hammer Collection

Through May 27, 2025

This impressive collection of European and American paintings and drawings reflects the interests and passion of the museum’s founder, Armand Hammer.
